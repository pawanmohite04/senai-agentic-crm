# Refund Policy

## Standard Refund Rule

Refunds are not available after 14 days from purchase or renewal except through an approved exception process. Support may offer service credits, onboarding help, or retention concessions when service quality contributed to customer harm.

## Exception Process

Refund exceptions require support manager approval and must include customer history, outage or defect evidence, prior response times, account value, and churn risk. Public review threats should be escalated to customer success leadership before any offer is made.

## Retention Playbook

For angry customers threatening cancellation or public reviews, acknowledge the delay, apologize for the experience, summarize the issue, assign a named owner, and consider a goodwill credit or discounted renewal. Do not promise a refund if the policy does not allow it.

## Chatbot Misinformation

If the company chatbot gave incorrect refund information, acknowledge the discrepancy, correct the policy, preserve evidence, and escalate internally. Avoid language that admits legal liability. Offer a human review of the account and document the chatbot statement versus the actual refund policy.
